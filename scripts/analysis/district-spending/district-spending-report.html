District Spending Trends Across the Merger

Rio Texas Annual Conference · apportionments by district, 2014–2024

Re-expressed in the 2025 three-district map (North / Central / South) · first-cut analysis

The question

How has spending in each district moved over the last decade — when the districts themselves were redrawn in 2025? The 2025 reorganization collapsed seven historic districts into three. To draw a continuous trend, every figure here is mapped into the new three-district frame using the official church-by-church crosswalk.

What we found

Most of the district decline is churches leaving, not surviving churches giving less. When we hold the cohort fixed to the 201 congregations that survived into the new map, the apportionment lines are comparatively flat. The steep drop in the all-churches line — about $1.82M between 2023 and 2024 — is overwhelmingly disaffiliation: the continuing cohort fell only $-0.06M over the same span. The merger obscures this; separating the two cohorts reveals it.

Two measures are reported, each split two ways:

  • Conference apportionment — each church's full apportionment to the conference. Re-bucketed exactly from per-church records.
  • District apportionment — money apportioned specifically for district missions (journal lines 28b/29b). Available only as old-district totals, so split into new districts by each old district's actual dollar flows.
  • All churches (solid) vs continuing cohort (dashed): the gap is what disaffiliation and closure removed.

Method & caveats

  • Sources. Per-church apportionment from the GCFA local-church statistical tables (2014–2024). Per-old-district district-missions figures hand-verified from the conference statistical recap in the 2022–2024 journals. Church-to-new-district crosswalk from the official 07.14.2025 three-district roster.
  • Data-year alignment. The journal recap reports two years in arrears of the volume; calibrated to the dollar against GCFA totals (e.g. the 2024 journal's recap = data year 2023).
  • 2024 is real, not partial. All 201 continuing churches report in 2024; the lower total reflects disaffiliated churches no longer reporting — which is the central finding, not a gap.
  • Unassigned ≈ 5%. A small slice of apportionment comes from churches in counties outside the new three-district map (mostly former Rio Grande / far-south congregations now closed or departed); excluded from district totals, noted in validation.
  • District apportionment is split-estimated. A planned per-church parse of the journal statistical tables would make this measure exact and extend the cohort lines.
